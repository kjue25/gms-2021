Sea Otters

Sea otters are the adorable, furry marine animals often seen floating on their back in the ocean. They are native to the northern and eastern coasts of The North Pacific Ocean. Adults typically weigh between 14 to 45 kg. While this makes them small among marine animals, they are actually the heaviest member of the weasel family. When they sleep, they often hold hands to prevent from floating away.

Baby Sea Otters

Newborns fur traps so much air, that they can't dive under water, so they often float and lay on their mothers since they can't swim. When their mothers leave for hunting, they rap their pups in kelp.They are born with their eyes open, teeth already amerging.
