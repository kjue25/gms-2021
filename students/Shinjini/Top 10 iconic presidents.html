Top 5 Iconic U.S. Presidents

Note: the Presidents are not in order.


    1. George Washington

    George Washington was the 1st President of the United States of America. He was known for being the “Father of His (or Our) Country.” He not only was the first president of the United States, but he also commanded the Continental Army during the American Revolution (1775–83) and guided the convention that outlined the U.S. Constitution.

    2. Abraham Lincoln

    Abraham Lincoln was the 16th President of the United States of America. Lincoln is famous for writing the Gettysburg Address, abolishing slavery (by participating in creating the Thirteenth Amendment), and being one of the 4 Presidents of US History to be assassinated.

    3. Franklin Delano Roosevelt (FDR)

    FDR was the 32nd President of the United States of America. Roosevelt is known for heading the Federal Government during the majority of the Great Depression, using a new domestic agenda he created called New Deal. As a result of his agenda, he reduced the biggest domestic crisis in U.S. history. In 1921, FDR was diagnosed with poliomyelitis (aka polio).

    4. Barack Obama

    Barack Obama was the 44th U.S. President. He is known for being the first African American U.S. President, and he helped the States pull through during the Great Recession of 2008. He founded ObamaCare soon after.
